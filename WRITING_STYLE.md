# Writing Style Guide

Rules for all written content in this project — docs, comments, messages, PRs, READMEs. Applies to humans and AI assistants equally.

## Voice

Direct and concise. First sentence says what the reader needs to know.

Professional but conversational — reads like a sharp person explaining, not a formal document.

State things clearly. No hedging ("this approach works well" not "this approach might potentially work well in some cases").

Short sentences for emphasis. Longer ones only when the idea demands it.

Trust the reader — don't over-explain or restate what's obvious from context.

## Word choices

Contractions are fine and preferred ("don't", "can't", "it's").

Plain language over jargon. Avoid "leverage", "utilize", "synergy", "circle back", "deep dive", "deliverables" — say what you mean instead.

Active voice. "The service handles retries" not "retries are handled by the service."

Concrete over vague: numbers, timeframes, specifics ("responds in <50ms", "ships in 3 weeks") instead of ("fast", "soon", "significant improvement").

## Structure

Lead with the point — what changed, what's needed, what the thing does. Context and rationale come after, not before.

Say what needs to be said, then stop. No padding, no restating the same point in different words.

Prose over bullet points unless the content is genuinely a list (steps, options, requirements). Don't use bullets where a sentence would flow better.

When a next step or action is needed, state it simply at the end.

## Tone

Confident without arrogant. State facts plainly — no superlatives, no underselling.

Warm but efficient — not cold, just not wasteful with words.

Flag uncertainty honestly ("unclear whether X — needs investigation") rather than burying it in soft language.

## What to avoid

Filler openers ("In this document we will discuss...", "As mentioned previously...").

Excessive qualifiers ("I think maybe", "it could potentially", "it's worth noting that").

Buzzwords and consultant-speak.

Restating what the reader already knows or can see from context.

Apologetic tone when none is warranted.

Emojis in documentation (UI labels and profile sections are exceptions).
